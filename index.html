Welcome to Our Campaign

We aim to raise awareness about discrimination against journalists and human rights defenders. Freedom of speech is a fundamental right that must be protected.

Every day, journalists face threats, censorship, and violence simply for reporting the truth. Governments, corporations, and extremist groups often attempt to silence those who challenge their actions. We must unite to defend press freedom and human rights.

Why This Matters

Journalists and human rights defenders are often targeted for exposing corruption and injustice. Without their work, many critical issues would remain hidden from the public.

We must take action to ensure their safety and uphold press freedom.

Discrimination against journalists takes many forms, including wrongful imprisonment, harassment, digital surveillance, and even murder. In many countries, perpetrators go unpunished, which perpetuates a cycle of fear and self-censorship.

Without a free press, democracy suffers, misinformation spreads, and societies become vulnerable to authoritarian control. This is why protecting journalists is not just about their safety—it’s about preserving truth and justice for all.

About SDG 16.10

SDG 16.10 focuses on ensuring public access to information and protecting fundamental freedoms. It is a crucial part of the United Nations' 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.

However, achieving this goal is difficult due to threats against journalists and activists. Many face violence, imprisonment, or even death for speaking out.

To combat this, we must advocate for stronger legal protections, hold perpetrators accountable, and support independent media organizations.

International organizations such as Reporters Without Borders and the Committee to Protect Journalists work tirelessly to document abuses and push for global policies that safeguard press freedom. Governments must also step up by enforcing laws that protect journalists and punishing those responsible for crimes against them.

Furthermore, digital platforms play a significant role in modern journalism. While they offer opportunities for information sharing, they also pose new challenges, such as cyber harassment and government surveillance. Striking a balance between security and free speech is essential.

One of the key ways to support SDG 16.10 is by promoting media literacy and ensuring that the public understands the importance of independent journalism. When societies value and protect journalists, they help create an informed, engaged, and empowered population that can hold governments accountable.

Additionally, civil society organizations, legal institutions, and the international community must collaborate to pressure oppressive regimes and fight against censorship. Supporting journalists through emergency funds, safe reporting channels, and legal aid is crucial in ensuring they can continue their work without fear.

Every individual has a role to play. Whether it’s supporting ethical journalism, speaking out against misinformation, or advocating for policy changes, everyone can contribute to the protection of fundamental freedoms and the right to information.

The Human Rights Defenders Summit 1998 put the issue of the safety and protection of HRDs on the map for governments, donor agencies, international NGOs and regional mechanisms. Since 1998, the world of support for HRDs has expanded profoundly and institutional space has been created to address HRD issues, including the establishment of the mandate for the UN Special Rapporteur on the situation of human rights defenders and similar regional mechanisms, within national governments and institutions, and among the international human rights movement. The 1998 Summit helped create the world in which both HRDs work and in which an array of institutions, foundations, and organizations engage with HRDs.

How we can stop discrimination against journalists and protect human rights defenders

The reason most journalists and human rights defenders are killed and discriminated is because their occupation comes with an inherent danger. This is because journalists and human rights defenders speak up about crimes and distribute information that's classified and could harm the reputation of countries or people.

Solutions we can take to stop discrimination

  • Breaking the cycle of Impunity
  • Put a global focus on violence against journalists

Breaking the cycle of Impunity

For many cases regarding crimes and violence directed towards journalists, the people who cause these crimes aren't held responsible or accountable for them. We believe that the more these criminals aren't held accountable for these crimes the more they'll commit them because they'll believe that there are no consequences to their actions. Furthermore, this cycle of criminals affirming their actions because of the impunity of society will only continue working to normalize the discrimination of journalists. For example, 90% of of cases in which journalists are murdered, those responsible are not held to account. However, actions we could take to end impunity are speaking up about crimes against journalists and advocating for their safety.

Putting a global focus on violence against journalists.

As conflict continues to grow nationally the death of journalists also continue to grow. This is because journalists are often targeted when reporting from conflict zones. The situation needs to be addressed globally because these countries aren't held accountable for the deaths they cause and violence they commit during times of war. Furthermore, if the situation is not addressed it enables these countries to continue their violence against human rights defenders, and journalists.
